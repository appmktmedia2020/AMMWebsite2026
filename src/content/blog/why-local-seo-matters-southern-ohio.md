If someone in Chillicothe searches "best HVAC company near me" or "dentist in Portsmouth Ohio," will they find your business — or your competitor?

That's what local SEO comes down to. And for small businesses in our region, it's the single highest-return marketing investment you can make.

## What Is Local SEO?

Local SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is the process of making your business more visible in location-based searches on Google. This includes:

- Showing up in the **Google Map Pack** (the 3 businesses that appear on a map at the top of search results)
- Ranking in regular search results for local keywords like "attorney in Portsmouth Ohio"
- Keeping your **Google Business Profile** accurate and active

When someone searches with location intent — and most local searches do — Google shows businesses it trusts are relevant, close by, and legitimate. Local SEO is how you become one of those trusted businesses.

## Why It Matters More Here

In larger markets, businesses have bigger advertising budgets and more competition. But in Southern Ohio, Eastern Kentucky, and West Virginia, many small businesses still haven't fully claimed their local presence online.

That means the playing field is more level — and the opportunity is bigger.

A plumber in Scioto County who invests in local SEO today can realistically dominate search results in their market within 6–12 months. That's not as easy to say about a plumber in Columbus competing with 200 other companies.

## The Foundation: Your Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is free real estate on the most visited website in the world. A fully optimized profile includes:

- **Accurate NAP** (Name, Address, Phone) — consistency across every directory matters
- **Business hours** kept up to date, including holidays
- **Photos** — businesses with photos get significantly more clicks
- **Regular posts** to signal that you're active
- **Review responses** — replying to every review, good or bad

If you haven't touched your GBP in the last 90 days, you're leaving visibility on the table.

## Citations: The Trust Signals You Don't See

A citation is any mention of your business name, address, and phone number across the web — Yelp, Facebook, Apple Maps, Yellow Pages, and dozens of industry-specific directories.

Google uses citation consistency as a trust signal. If your address is listed differently across 30 directories, that confusion can quietly suppress your local rankings. Cleaning up and building citations is foundational SEO work that most businesses skip — and most agencies don't prioritize.

At AMM, we track citations for all of our local SEO clients and make sure they're consistent across every major platform.

## What to Expect From Local SEO

Local SEO isn't instant, but it compounds over time. Here's a realistic timeline:

- **Month 1–2:** Foundation work — GBP optimization, citation cleanup, keyword research
- **Month 3–4:** You start appearing for more search terms; Map Pack impressions increase
- **Month 5–6:** Consistent traffic from local searches; early ranking improvements visible in BrightLocal reports
- **Month 6–12:** Compounding growth as reviews build and content matures

The businesses that win local search aren't the ones who tried it for a month. They're the ones who stayed consistent.
